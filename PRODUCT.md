# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Insertrice primarily serves students and young graduates in Benin who need practical guidance when choosing a career direction. They may have limited access to professional orientation services and need recommendations that feel understandable, credible, and locally relevant.

The product is designed to expand later to young people across francophone Africa. Its structure must accommodate additional guidance, learning, and career-development features without losing clarity.

## Product Purpose

Insertrice helps users understand their interests, DISC profile, and competencies, then translates those signals into realistic career recommendations and actionable next steps.

Success means a user finishes the diagnostic with greater confidence, understands why the recommended careers fit, and knows what to do next. The product must make a consequential decision feel guided rather than intimidating.

## Brand Personality

Encouraging, credible, empowering.

The experience should combine Duolingo's approachable guidance, LinkedIn's professional credibility, and Notion's interface clarity. It should speak directly and respectfully, using plain French and concrete recommendations.

## Anti-references

- Corporate stiffness that makes orientation feel bureaucratic or inaccessible.
- Childish gamification that trivializes career decisions.
- Excessive decoration that competes with the user's task.
- Generic AI or SaaS styling with interchangeable cards, gradients, and empty claims.
- Dense professional jargon or unexplained scoring.

## Design Principles

1. **Guide one decision at a time.** Each screen should make the next action obvious and keep cognitive load low.
2. **Explain the recommendation.** Scores and career matches must be understandable, not presented as opaque authority.
3. **Earn trust through clarity.** Use consistent patterns, concrete language, and visible progress rather than decorative polish alone.
4. **Encourage without infantilizing.** The tone can be warm and motivating while respecting the importance of the user's choices.
5. **Design for growth.** New features should fit a coherent product system rather than becoming isolated experiences.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Support keyboard navigation, visible focus states, sufficient color contrast, color-blind-safe status indicators, semantic markup, and reduced-motion preferences.

Use accessible, plain French first. Keep the language and examples adaptable for future francophone African markets without assuming every user shares the same educational, economic, or cultural context.
